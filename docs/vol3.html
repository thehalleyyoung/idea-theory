Volume III — Idea Theory and the Humanities

Scope. Hermeneutics, intertextuality, dialectical reading, literary tradition, translation, and the history of ideas.

Background from the Literature

Hegelian dialectic (Hegel 1812) supplies the prototype for T3 (Aufhebung). Gadamer's (1960) fusion of horizons motivates T4 (emergence in interpretation). Kristeva's (1969) intertextuality and Bloom's (1973) anxiety of influence find natural homes in T1 (composition) and T7 (transmission chains). Jakobson (1959) and Benjamin (1923) on translation map onto T6 (conjugation). Lévi-Strauss (1958) on structural equivalence in myth maps onto T8.
