The Nine Theorems

Volume I proves nine purely mathematical theorems. Each is a Lean 4 theorem in the IdeaTheory namespace, with a docstring citing the informal claim and source. Volumes II–VI interpret these theorems but add no further mathematical content to the foundation.

#TheoremLean fileStatement (informal)
T1Idea composition monoidIdeaTheory/CompositionMonoid.leanIdeas under composition form a monoid; associativity and identity hold.
T2Resonance bilinear pairingIdeaTheory/ResonancePairing.leanR extends to a bilinear form on the free real module on ideas.
T3Aufhebung decompositionIdeaTheory/Aufhebung.leanEvery idea factors uniquely (up to ~) into preserved, negated, and sublated parts.
T4Emergence 2-cocycleIdeaTheory/EmergenceCocycle.leanκ satisfies the 2-cocycle identity; coboundaries correspond to "trivial" emergence.
T5Meaning curvature formIdeaTheory/MeaningCurvature.leanγ = R − Rᵀ is antisymmetric and obstructs commutativity of composition under R.
T6Conjugation actionIdeaTheory/Conjugation.leanConjugation by an invertible idea is a weight-preserving automorphism.
T7Transmission chainIdeaTheory/TransmissionChain.leanFor any chain x₀ → … → xₙ, weight propagates additively up to total curvature defect.
T8Structural equivalenceIdeaTheory/StructuralEquivalence.lean~ is a congruence; the quotient I/~ inherits all nine structures.
T9Graded idea algebraIdeaTheory/GradedAlgebra.leanThe free real module on I is an ℕ-graded associative algebra with respect to g, and R respects the grading.
