Volume VI — Advanced Applications and Unifying Perspectives

Scope. Category-theoretic, information-theoretic, and cross-domain syntheses; applications to artificial intelligence, scientific theory change, complex systems, and the philosophy of science.

Background from the Literature

Mac Lane (1971) and Lawvere (1969) provide the categorical lens; the idea monoid generalises to a monoidal category whose 2-cocycles are emergence terms. Shannon (1948) and Tononi (2008) supply information-theoretic invariants attached to the resonance pairing. Kuhn (1962) and Lakatos (1970) on scientific revolutions correspond to large jumps in the Aufhebung decomposition (T3) accompanied by non-trivial cocycles (T4). Marcus (2020) on AI compositionality and Bengio (2021) on systematicity in deep learning provide modern cognitive-architecture applications.
