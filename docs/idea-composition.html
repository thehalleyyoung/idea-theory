idea composition

Home · Theorems · Idea Composition
Idea composition is the binary operation ∙ : I × I → I on the set of ideas I that yields the graded idea monoid (I, ∙, e) of Theorem 1. It formalizes the recurring observation, from Locke and Hume through Frege and contemporary memetics, that ideas combine into compound ideas while preserving an underlying notion of weight or grade.

Definition

structure IdeaMonoid where
  carrier : Type
  compose : carrier → carrier → carrier
  unit : carrier
  assoc : ∀ a b c, compose (compose a b) c = compose a (compose b c)
  left_id : ∀ a, compose unit a = a
  right_id: ∀ a, compose a unit = a

Headline theorem

Theorem 1 (Composition). (I, ∙, e) is a monoid; equipped with a grading w : I → ℕ satisfying w(a ∙ b) = w(a) + w(b) and w(e)=0 it is a graded monoid. ✓ verified
