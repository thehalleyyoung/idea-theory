Volume IV — Idea Theory, Cognitive Science, and Philosophy of Mind

Scope. Concept composition, mental representations, intentionality, modularity, and the binding problem.

Background from the Literature

Fodor & Pylyshyn (1988) argue that thought is systematic and compositional, which T1 makes precise. Smolensky's (1990) tensor-product representations match T2 (resonance bilinear pairing). Clark & Chalmers (1998) on the extended mind motivate T7 (transmission chains across cognitive scaffolds). Dennett's (1991) intentional stance and Millikan's (1984) teleosemantics map onto T6 (conjugation as reframing under interpretive stance). Marr's (1982) tri-level analysis and Simon's (1962) hierarchy correspond to T9.
