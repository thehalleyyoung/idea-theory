Volume I — Mathematical Foundations of Idea Theory

Scope. This volume develops the nine core theorems of Idea Theory as basic mathematics. No interpretive commitments are made here; those are deferred to Volumes II–VI.

Background from the Literature

The mathematical signature of Idea Theory abstracts and unifies several pre-existing structures: monoidal models of concept combination (Sperber 1996); bilinear pairings on cultural-symbol spaces (Lotman 1990); Hegelian Aufhebung (Hegel 1812); group-cohomological 2-cocycles in the theory of central extensions; antisymmetric forms in symplectic geometry; congruences in universal algebra (Birkhoff 1935); and ℕ-gradings in homological algebra. Volume I shows that all nine structures coexist consistently on a single carrier.
