Transmission Chains

An iterated composition \(a_1\circ a_2\circ\cdots\circ a_n\) is a transmission chain. The cumulative emergence telescopes by the cocycle identity.

Definition. A chain in \(\mathcal{I}\) is a finite sequence \(a_1,\ldots,a_n\); its composite is \(a_1\circ\cdots\circ a_n\). For a valuation \(v:\mathcal{I}\to A\), the cumulative cocycle is \[ \Omega(a_1,\ldots,a_n) := v(a_1\circ\cdots\circ a_n) - \sum_{i} v(a_i). \]

By Theorem 7, \(\Omega = \sum_{i<j} \omega(a_i, a_{i+1}\circ\cdots\circ a_j)\) up to a coboundary; in particular, \(\Omega\) is well-defined modulo bracketing.

Worked example: cultural transmission

Boyd & Richerson model cultural inheritance as a transmission chain in which each generation composes received material with novel input. The cumulative emergence \(\Omega\) is what distinguishes a ratchet (\(\Omega\) strictly accumulating) from drift (\(\Omega\) bounded). See Boyd–Richerson, Henrich.

Derived Primitives Anchored Here

The following derived primitives in the 10-tuple algebra are constructed using this concept as a structural ingredient:

  • τ — Temporality. A temporal subcategory of \(\mathcal{H}\) is a chain of horizons, and \(\tau_\Delta=\xi(f_{t,t+\Delta})\) is the translation along one of its links. A biography, proof, or cultural lineage becomes temporal when its stages are organized as composable steps.
  • μ — Credence. Credence updates are compatible probability sections pushed along chain steps, \(\mu_{h'}=\xi(g)_*\mu_h\). Bayesian learning is therefore a transmission chain in which each observation moves the measure to the next horizon.
  • π — Perspective. Chain endpoints can serve as agent perspectives because a pointed connected subcategory records where an agent starts and which horizon steps are accessible. A dialogue links two perspectives when a chain of translations carries claims from one home horizon to the other.

Chains make process visible without adding a separate process primitive. Time, belief revision, and perspective-taking can be derived by selecting different chain structures inside \(\mathcal{H}\) and reading them through \(\xi\). The methodological choice is whether to treat the path itself as primitive or to expose it as composable transmission.
