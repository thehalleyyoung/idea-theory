Composition

Composition is the operation \(\circ:\mathcal{I}\times\mathcal{I}\to\mathcal{I}\). It is associative and has a two-sided identity \(e\).

Definition. A composition on \(\mathcal{I}\) is a function \(\circ\) such that for all \(a,b,c\in\mathcal{I}\), \((a\circ b)\circ c=a\circ(b\circ c)\), and there exists \(e\in\mathcal{I}\) with \(e\circ a=a\circ e=a\).

Composition encodes the simplest fact about ideas: that they can be put together. Locke's simple versus complex ideas are exactly the generators and words of a free composition. Frege's principle of compositionality says that the meaning of a composite is determined by the meanings of the parts and the way they are combined — i.e. by the structure of \(\circ\).

Worked example

Let \(\mathcal{I}\) be the set of grammatical phrases of English, with concatenation as composition and the empty phrase as \(e\). The result is non-commutative — "white wedding" is not "wedding white" — but associative and unital. The free monoid on the alphabet of words is the syntactic idea algebra; it has no semantic structure yet. Resonance and Aufhebung add the semantics.

Why it is non-trivial

The monoid axioms are weak but not vacuous. They rule out the conflation \(a\circ a = a\) (idempotency, which would make every idea its own composite with itself, true in some semilattice models but false in general). They also rule out the strict commutativity \(a\circ b=b\circ a\), which makes the order in which ideas are combined invisible — patently false for narrative, argument, and most concept formation.

Derived Primitives Anchored Here

The following derived primitives in the 10-tuple algebra are constructed using this concept as a structural ingredient:

  • R — Reflexion. Self-application is composition turned back on the algebra itself, so the fixed point \(\mathrm{Fix}(\xi\mapsto\xi(\xi))\) depends on being able to compose a structure with its own representation. A Gödel sentence or strange loop is formally a composite whose interpretation includes the very operation that produced it.
  • β — Rigid reference. A rigid designator is stable through horizon-translations because the same item remains identifiable up to identity and composition-preserving transport. For example, the name \(b_{\text{Aristotle}}\) behaves rigidly only if composing it with descriptions across possible horizons still points back to one section of \(\xi\).
  • λ — Potentiality. Potentiality uses future refinements of an idea, and those refinements are tracked through the ways new composites can be formed from the present idea. A seed concept has \(\lambda_h(I)\) beyond its current resonance because later horizons may compose it with new background ideas.

Composition therefore explains why some philosophers treat reflexion, reference, and potentiality as primitive while others derive them from a more basic algebra of combination. Once the horizon category and the monoid operation are fixed, the available fixed points, rigid sections, and future composites are determined together. The primitive/derived choice is a methodological decision about whether to expose or hide this compositional machinery.
