Meaning Curvature

The antisymmetric part \(\kappa(a,b) = \tfrac12(\rho(a,b)-\rho(b,a))\) of the resonance pairing is the meaning curvature.

Definition. Let \(\rho\) be a resonance pairing. The meaning curvature is \(\kappa(a,b) := \tfrac12(\rho(a,b)-\rho(b,a))\). It is a 2-form on \(\mathbb{R}\langle\mathcal{I}\rangle\); if it is closed (in the sense of \(d\kappa = 0\) under composition), its de Rham class is invariant.

Curvature measures the failure of resonance to be symmetric. In language: "salt and pepper" but rarely "pepper and salt"; "Romeo and Juliet" but rarely "Juliet and Romeo". The directional asymmetry is not noise; it is a global topological invariant of the algebra.

Worked example: Derrida's différance

Derrida's claim that meaning is constituted by deferral and difference can be read as the assertion that \(\kappa\neq 0\) on the algebra of signifiers — that no symmetric semantic relation captures meaning, because meaning is irreducibly directed. (See Derrida entry.)

Derived Primitives Anchored Here

The following derived primitives in the 10-tuple algebra are constructed using this concept as a structural ingredient:

  • ψ — Signed valence. Signed valence subtracts the resonance of the antisymmetric component from the symmetric one, so its negative pole traces \(\kappa\)-like directional tension. An idea can repel an agent when “self toward idea” and “idea toward self” curve differently.
  • ι — Intentionality. Directional aboutness inherits curvature whenever \(\rho_h(I,J)\) and \(\rho_h(J,I)\) diverge. A command, promise, or accusation is intentional in a way that cannot be captured by a symmetric relation between speaker and content.
  • δ — Differentiation. Curvature helps locate differential contrasts because asymmetric resonance can make one direction salient while the reverse direction fails to preserve the same structure. Framing effects arise when “gain versus loss” and “loss versus gain” occupy different positions in the differential field.

Here \(\kappa\) is meaning curvature from T5, not the horizon functor \(\xi\). Its methodological role is to mark where symmetric accounts of meaning, preference, or inference break down. Many T14-style obstructions can be read as failures to flatten this curvature, so primitives like valence or framing become derivable from antisymmetric resonance rather than separately postulated.
