conjugation of ideas

Home · Theorems · Conjugation
The conjugation action of Theorem 6 sends an idea a and an invertible context g to g ∙ a ∙ g⁻¹. It captures the informal intuition that the “same” idea, transposed into another discursive frame, is not literally the same element of I but the transform of one element by another. Conjugation makes the orbit structure of ideas precise and underlies the equivalence theory of Volume III.

Definition

def conj (M : IdeaMonoid) (g a gInv : M.carrier) : M.carrier :=
  M.compose (M.compose g a) gInv
