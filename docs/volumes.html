The Six Volumes

Volume I is the mathematical core: nine theorems, no extra-mathematical commitments. Volumes II–VI each interpret the same nine theorems in a different domain, with full literature bridges.

VolumeTitleTheme
IMathematical Foundations of Idea TheoryPure mathematics: monoid, pairing, decomposition, cocycle, curvature, conjugation, chains, equivalence, grading.
IIIdea Theory and the Social SciencesNorms, institutions, opinion dynamics, cultural transmission, social conflict.
IIIIdea Theory and the HumanitiesHermeneutics, intertextuality, dialectical reading, literary tradition.
IVIdea Theory, Cognitive Science, and Philosophy of MindConcept composition, mental representations, intentionality, modularity.
VEmergence: A Formal TheoryThe emergence 2-cocycle as a rigorous account of strong/weak emergence.
VIAdvanced Applications and Unifying PerspectivesCategory-theoretic, information-theoretic, and cross-domain syntheses.

Each volume page includes: Background from the Literature, a From Informal Claims to Formal Statements table mapping prose claims to the Lean theorems, and Further Reading.
