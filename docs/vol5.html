Volume V — Emergence: A Formal Theory

Scope. The "problem" of emergence — how a whole can be more than the sum of its parts — recast as the cohomology class of the emergence 2-cocycle κ.

Background from the Literature

Broad (1925) distinguished mechanistic from emergent properties. Bedau (1997, 2008) introduced "weak emergence" as derivability in principle but not in practice. Kim (1999) raised the spectre of downward causation. Humphreys (1997) called for a formal theory of fusion. Idea Theory answers: weak emergence corresponds to coboundary cocycles, strong emergence to non-trivial cohomology classes.

Headline result. Emergence is real iff the emergence cocycle is cohomologically non-trivial. This is a precise mathematical statement, formalised in Lean as IdeaTheory.kappa_cocycle together with the cohomology machinery on the graded idea algebra (T9).
