Theorems

The nine foundational theorems of Idea Theory. Each is machine-checked under lean/IdeaTheory/Theorems{1..12}.lean.

  • Theorem 1: Composition\((\mathcal{I},\circ,e)\) is a monoid: identity is unique and elements with two-sided inverses are cancellative.
  • Theorem 2: Resonance Pairing\(\rho\) extends uniquely to a bilinear form on \(\mathbb{R}\langle\mathcal{I}\rangle\); non-degeneracy is preserved by composition.
  • Theorem 3: Aufhebung DecompositionFor every \(a,b\in\mathcal{I}\), the decomposition \(a\circ b = \sigma(a,b)+\nu(a,b)+\eta(a,b)\) exists and is unique modulo \(\ker\rho\).
  • Theorem 4: Emergence 2-Cocycle\(\omega\) is a normalized 2-cocycle: \(\partial\omega=0\) and \(\omega(e,\cdot)=\omega(\cdot,e)=0\).
  • Theorem 5: Meaning CurvatureThe antisymmetric part \(\kappa\) of \(\rho\) is closed; its cohomology class is a structural invariant.
  • Theorem 6: ConjugationFor any invertible \(g\in\mathcal{I}\), the inner automorphism \(\mathrm{Ad}_g(a) = g\circ a\circ g^{-1}\) preserves \(\rho,\sigma,\nu,\omega\).
  • Theorem 7: Transmission ChainFor a chain \(a_1,\ldots,a_n\), the cumulative cocycle telescopes.
  • Theorem 8: Structural EquivalenceTwo idea algebras are isomorphic iff there is a graded monoid isomorphism preserving \(\rho,\sigma,\nu,\omega\).
  • Theorem 9: Grading\(\deg\) extends to a homomorphism into \(G\) compatible with \(\rho\), Aufhebung, and \(\omega\).
