Idea Theory: The Book

A ten-chapter mathematical monograph developing idea algebras from first principles, with machine-verified proofs in Lean 4 and a zoo of forty-plus historical positions.

Each chapter builds on the previous, starting from the three bare monoid axioms and progressively adding structure: resonance, Aufhebung, coalitions, emergence cocycle, conjugation, transmission chains, structural equivalence, grading. Every theorem is verified in IdeaTheory.Theorems{1..9}; every chapter closes with a zoo of historical positions formalized in the chapter's vocabulary.

ChapterTitle~WordsSummary
1 The Idea Monoid 7,500 Three axioms — associativity and a two-sided unit — are isolated and defended on philosophical grounds. From them the book derives powers, chains, sub-ideals, idempotents, cancellation, and invertibility, with three headline theorems and four corollaries. The chapter closes with a zoo matching Locke, Hume, Frege, and Wittgenstein against the bare monoid.
2 Coalitions, Committees, and the Algebra of Aggregation 7,500 The free-list monad is used to build coalitions and the aggregate operator, turning finite collections of ideas into a single idea. Three headline theorems — coalition associativity, silent-agent invariance, and social congruence — are proved, and six historical positions (Granovetter, Olson, Latour, Bourdieu, Sawyer, Condorcet) are formalized.
3 The Aufhebung Decomposition 7,500 Two endofunctions \(\sigma\) and \(\nu\) on the carrier formalize Hegel's dialectical triple of preservation, negation, and elevation. Five axioms yield an exact decomposition \(\sigma(a)\circ\nu(a)=a\), idempotent stabilization, and a fixed-point theorem. Seven zoo entries span Hegel, Marx, Adorno, Fauconnier–Turner, Koestler, Deleuze, and Derrida.
4 The Resonance Pairing 7,500 A real-valued bilinear pairing \(\rho:\mathcal{I}\times\mathcal{I}\to\mathbb{R}\) is introduced, measuring how well two ideas "fit." The pairing extends uniquely to the free vector space on \(\mathcal{I}\), and non-degeneracy survives composition. Zoo entries formalize Husserl's noemata, Fodor's LOT, and semantic-vector models of cognition.
5 The Emergence Cocycle 7,500 The 2-cocycle \(\omega(a,b)\) captures the irreducible novelty produced by composition: the discrepancy between a valuation of a composite and the sum of individual valuations. The cocycle condition \(\partial\omega=0\) is proved, and the cohomology class is shown to be a structural invariant. Zoo entries formalize Deleuze's virtual problems, emergent collective intelligence, and inter-level causation.
6 Conjugation and Reframing 7,500 Invertible ideas act on the algebra by conjugation \({}^g\!a = g\circ a\circ g^{-1}\), yielding inner automorphisms that preserve every piece of structure. The group of units acts as a symmetry group; Kantian categories are formalized as conjugation operators. Zoo entries cover Kant's schemata, Kuhn's paradigm shifts, and Foucault's epistemic frames.
7 Transmission Chains and Cultural Attractors 7,500 Iterating composition along a chain of carriers telescopes: cumulative emergence is a sum of pairwise cocycle terms. Cultural attractors appear as fixed points of the iterated composition followed by resonance-norm truncation. Zoo entries formalize Sperber's epidemiology of representations, Dawkins's memes, and cumulative cultural evolution.
8 Structural Equivalence 7,500 Two idea algebras are isomorphic exactly when there exists a graded monoid isomorphism preserving \(\rho\), \(\sigma\), \(\nu\), and \(\omega\). Translation indeterminacy (Quine) is shown to be non-uniqueness of the structural isomorphism; conceptual relativity is the existence of inequivalent but internally consistent algebras over the same carrier.
9 Grading 7,500 A degree map \(\deg:\mathcal{I}\to G\) into a totally ordered abelian group \(G\) is introduced and shown to extend to a homomorphism compatible with \(\rho\), \(\sigma\), \(\nu\), and \(\omega\). Levels exist and stack; the graded structure formalizes complexity hierarchies, conceptual depth, and Bourdieu's volume of capital.
10 Philosophical Synthesis 7,500 The nine foundational theorems are assembled into a single unified structure theorem for idea algebras. The full formalism is reviewed against thirty additional zoo entries spanning philosophy, cognitive science, sociology, economics, and literary theory. Prospects for Volume II — the social and empirical applications — are outlined.

How to read the book

Each chapter is self-contained at the level of definitions: all notation is introduced where used. However, the theorems in later chapters rely on results proved in earlier ones. A reader interested only in the philosophical zoo may read the final section of each chapter independently, with the brief formal restatements serving as entry points into the algebra. A reader interested in the algebra should read Chapters 1–3 in sequence before branching to any of Chapters 4–9.

Every result cited as "(Verified as IdeaTheory.X)" has a machine-checked proof in the Lean 4 source repository. The Lean source is browsable under Lean.
