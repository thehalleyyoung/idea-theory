# Chapter 6 — Meaning Curvature: The Antisymmetric Residue

Idea Theory begins with a few operating names. Composition is the act of putting ideas together. The resonance pairing measures how one idea calls, resists, or illuminates another. Each composition has a preservation part, a suppression part, and an emergence part. The emergence cocycle records how novelty behaves across regrouping. Degree or grading measures articulation. Meaning curvature names the durable asymmetry in resonance. Context enters through the horizon category, and through the horizon functor — the rule mathematicians label with the Greek letter xi — which assigns a full idea algebra to each context.

## The question in plain speech

Meaning curvature names the part of resonance that refuses reciprocity. If one idea calls another differently than the second calls the first, there is a bend in the semantic field. The formal text calls this the antisymmetric residue, but for the ear the phrase meaning curvature is better. It says that meaning can twist. The path from patient to doctor is not the same as the path from doctor to patient. The path from colony to empire is not the same as the path from empire to colony.

Because this book is written for the ear, you do not need to hold a diagram in your head. Imagine instead a small workshop. Ideas arrive at the bench. Some can be joined without strain. Some scrape against one another. Some cancel pieces of one another. Some make a third thing appear, a thing that neither ingredient carried alone. Around the workshop are rooms, and each room is a horizon: a language game, a historical moment, a scientific paradigm, a social role, a possible world, or a private point of view. Moving rooms changes what can be said, what can be inferred, and what counts as the same idea.

## A scene to carry

Consider apology and power. A child apologizing to a teacher, a government apologizing to a harmed minority, and a friend apologizing to a friend all use a related act. Yet power bends the resonance. Apology may call forgiveness, humiliation, strategy, repair, or public relations depending on direction. The apologizer's meaning and the receiver's meaning are not mirror images. Meaning curvature names that nonmirror relation as a durable feature rather than as a mere misunderstanding.

## What the structure keeps honest

The structure keeps us from smoothing away asymmetry. Many theories of meaning begin with similarity or shared reference. Those are real, but they miss the one-way force of many ideas. Cause resonates toward effect differently than effect resonates toward cause. Command resonates toward obedience differently than obedience resonates toward command. Trauma resonates toward memory differently than memory resonates toward trauma. Meaning curvature says that some asymmetries are not noise. They are structural invariants of the algebra.

## Older conversations inside the new vocabulary

Cognitive science studies framing effects, where equivalent descriptions lead to different judgments. Distributional semantics learns that ratios and contrasts can carry more meaning than raw co-occurrence. Hegelian dialectic hears force in one-sided dependence, where master and servant do not mean one another symmetrically. Political theory hears curvature in domination, recognition, and voice. Idea Theory gathers these insights by saying: listen for the part of resonance that changes when the direction is reversed.



## How to listen for it

When you listen for meaning curvature, ask what happens if the relation is turned around. Teacher of student is not student of teacher. Critique of tradition is not tradition of critique. Love of country is not country of love. Sometimes the reversal is grammatical nonsense. Sometimes it is meaningful but changed. Sometimes it reveals an invisible hierarchy. The bend is not a defect in speech. It is one of the places meaning lives.

For the listener, the safest method is to hear the same distinction in several registers. In a sentence, ask what is being joined. In a conversation, ask what one speaker preserves from another and what is quietly suppressed. In a scientific dispute, ask which horizon makes an inference natural and which horizon breaks it. In a social movement, ask how the transmitted slogan changes as it passes from mouth to mouth. None of these questions requires symbolic notation. They require patient attention to roles.

## Chapter summary

The chapter's lesson is simple to say and slow to exhaust: Idea Theory is a small vocabulary for tracking composition, resonance, preservation, suppression, emergence, grading, curvature, and context without pretending that meaning floats free of use. Keep the role names in your ear. They are the handles by which the later examples will be lifted.
