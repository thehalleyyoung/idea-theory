# Chapter 71 — Late Wittgenstein

This chapter treats Late Wittgenstein as a test case for idea theory. We keep the operating
definitions audible: composition puts ideas together, the identity idea leaves a composition
unchanged, the resonance pairing measures directed evocation, the preservation part,
suppression part, and emergence part describe what is kept, cancelled, and newly produced,
and the horizon functor — the rule mathematicians label with the Greek letter xi — lets the
whole algebra vary by context.

## Biographical preamble

Ludwig Wittgenstein, pronounced approximately *LOOD-vig VIT-gen-shtine* lived from 1889 to
1951, worked in Austria and Britain, and belongs here to ordinary-language philosophy. In
one compressed sentence, his later watchword was that meaning is use. That sentence gives
the chapter its entry point: meaning is not a bare object sitting before a spectator, but a
structured achievement within a field of acts, signs, practices, or horizons.

## Position

The position can be put simply. The Philosophical Investigations (1953) repudiates the
picture theory of the Tractatus . Meaning is not a relation of word to object or proposition
to fact but a feature of use within a practice. To understand a word is to know how it is
used; to ask for its meaning apart from its use is to be misled by a grammatical picture.
Language games are the units of analysis: small, complete forms of language-with-action that
exhibit how words function. The slab-game of the builders, the giving-and-following of
orders, the reporting of pain — each is a language game with its own internal grammar. The
point is not merely historical. It tells us what kind of item is being counted as an idea:
not a loose mental image, but a content whose sense depends on the way it is given, used,
interpreted, or carried forward.

## Algebraic re-statement

In the algebraic re-statement, the basic domain is the field of contents named by the zoo
entry. Take the idea domain to be the set of language games: practices in which words and
actions are interwoven into complete forms of life. Each element is tagged by the game it
belongs to, the role it plays in that game, and the criteria of correct use that govern it.
The identity idea is the empty practice-position — the slot a contribution to a language
game fills. Composition is language-game combination: the way smaller games are interwoven
into larger forms of life. composition is constrained by the forms of life themselves; not
every combination is a recognizable practice, and the constraints are themselves part of the
form of life. The resonance pairing is family resemblance: an overlap measure of features
shared between practices, with no requirement that any single feature be common to all. Read
aloud, this says that the thinker supplies a local model of the ten-tuple: a domain of
contents, a composition rule, an identity idea, directed resonance, the threefold division
into preservation, suppression, and emergence, an emergence cocycle, a degree of
articulation, and a context-sensitive horizon structure.

## Theorem re-statement

*The theorem-style claim is this: The first theorem-shaped claim is that meaning is use: there is no element of the idea domain apart from its role in composition.*

In paragraph form, the claim rules out the fantasy that meaning is already complete before
context enters. The first theorem-shaped claim is that meaning is use: there is no element
of the idea domain apart from its role in composition. To pull a word out of its language
game and ask for its meaning in isolation is to encounter no meaning at all. This is a T1
claim with extreme holism: identity of an element is exhausted by its compositional role.
The second claim is that family resemblance is the resonance pairing as an overlap count,
not an essence. The prediction is that failures of understanding will not be accidental
noise. They will cluster where a local content cannot be carried unchanged across a change
of horizon, or where an apparent identity hides suppressed difference.

Carefully restated, the relevant idea algebra has a domain of contents, an identity idea,
associative composition, the resonance pairing, the preservation part, the suppression part,
the emergence part, the emergence cocycle, degree, the horizon category, and the horizon
functor. The central claim says that the philosopher's privileged object is stable only when
these roles cooperate. Where local data can be glued, the chapter instantiates T14; where
translation fails, the obstruction is also audible as a horizon-functor effect.

## What the formalism adds

What the formalism adds is clarification without replacement. First, family resemblance
becomes a precise resonance metric without essential properties. The intuition
that some concepts hang together without sharing an essence gains an algebraic correlate:
the resonance pairing is overlap-based rather than membership-in-a-class-based. Second, the
private language argument becomes the assertion that the idea domain requires a public
composition to be well-defined — a social condition on the monoid. The argument's structural
form is that without a public composition, there are no determinate elements to be composed.
Third, the rejection of essences is the structural claim that composition does not factor
through any privileged set of features. In ordinary language, the formalism lets us ask
which operation does the philosophical work. Is the key relation resonance, because one
content fulfills or evokes another? Is it suppression, because meaning appears through
contrast? Is it the horizon functor, because every local act changes when the context
changes? The answer differs from thinker to thinker, but the vocabulary stays stable.

## Where it diverges

The divergence is equally important. First, the anti-theoretical stance of the
Investigations directly opposes building a theory; the formalism produces exactly the kind
of theory Wittgenstein II opposes. To present an algebra of language games risks performing
the very mistake the book diagnoses. Second, the piecemeal, conversational, dialectical
style of the Investigations is part of its content. The algebra's systematic exposition
flattens what Wittgenstein deliberately presents as a series of remarks intended to provoke
rather than to demonstrate. Third, the absence of theses in Wittgenstein's later philosophy
is in tension with the theorem-shaped claims the algebra invites. This is not a defect in
the reading. It marks the price of any formal reconstruction: the algebra shows the load-
bearing structure, while the original philosophy often also includes tone, method, ethical
urgency, religious practice, literary performance, or first-person discipline.

## Horizon structure and the horizon-functor theorem

The horizon structure names the context in which the local algebra lives. The later
Wittgenstein's the horizon category is the discrete-up-to-family-resemblance category of
language-games and forms of life. Morphisms are partial overlaps in use, training, and
criteria, not hidden common essences; meaning is intra-horizonal and public. schema or
paradigm is the form of life, perspective is the participant trained into rule-following,
differentiation is contrastive use, and private-language fantasy is a failed singleton
horizon. This is paradigmatic T14: cross-game inference fails, and the private-language
argument denies that any the horizon functor-section over an isolated private horizon is
even definable. For this reason the chapter should be heard through T14. T13 is the
hermeneutic sheaf theorem, where local meanings glue into a global one when they agree on
overlaps. T14 is the indexical pullback theorem, where preservation and emergence fail to
translate cleanly. T15 is the profundity theorem, where depth comes from breadth across
horizons and closure under emergence.

## Derived primitives

The derived primitives make the diagnosis compact. schema or paradigm: Language-games are
connected components, each giving rules for locally intelligible use. differentiation:
Meaning-as-use is differential, fixed by contrasts among practices rather than inner
objects. perspective: Form of life is the shared perspective from which rule-following
becomes possible. R. Philosophical therapy is reflexive, dissolving confusions generated by
our own language. None of these is introduced as an extra metaphysical ingredient. Each is
derived from the ten-tuple: intentionality from resonance across horizons, temporality from
ordered horizon-translation, perspective from a home region in the horizon category,
differentiation from suppression, and reflexion from self-application of the horizon
functor.

## Chapter summary

Late Wittgenstein therefore enters idea theory as a disciplined local algebra of meaning.
The chapter has followed the promised path: biography, position, algebraic re-statement,
theorem re-statement, addition, divergence, horizon structure, and derived primitives. The
result is not a replacement for the thinker, but a listening guide to the formal pressure
already present in the work.
