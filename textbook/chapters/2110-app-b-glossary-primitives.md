# Appendix B — Glossary of the Eleven Derived Primitives

Each primitive here is defined three times: one sentence, one paragraph, and one extended walk-through. The point is economy. These are not added knobs. They are ways of reading the horizon category, horizon functor, resonance, grading, composition, and emergence already present.

## Temporality

One sentence: time as a directed region of horizons.

One paragraph: A temporal process is an idea traced along ordered horizons, where earlier and later contexts are connected by disciplined restriction.

Extended walk-through: Imagine hearing a melody. No note alone is the melody, yet the melody is not outside the notes. Temporality names the ordered horizon path that lets earlier content remain available while later content changes what the earlier meant.

Input data: To derive temporality, begin with the ordinary core of Idea Theory rather than with a new faculty. Use horizons, horizon translations, resonance, composition, grading, and the decomposition into preservation, suppression, and emergence. The primitive is legitimate only if those materials suffice.

Construction: The construction asks what has to be selected or traced. Sometimes we select a subregion of horizons. Sometimes we trace a resonance profile. Sometimes we compare degrees across a refinement. Sometimes we look for a fixed point. The important point is that the operation is a reading of existing structure.

No smuggling check: If the explanation secretly assumes time, aboutness, viewpoint, desire, reference, probability, or selfhood before deriving it, it fails. If the explanation can point to the exact horizon structure and the exact resonance or grading behavior doing the work, it passes.

Common mistake: Do not treat temporality as a separate mental box. The whole point of calling it derived is that the familiar word names a pattern already present in the algebra. That pattern may be strong, weak, local, global, stable, or broken. Describing its strength is better than merely announcing that the primitive exists.

One paragraph: An idea is about something when its resonance profile points coherently toward objects, states, or possibilities across horizons.

If the pattern remains coherent across bodily, linguistic, and practical horizons, intentionality has been derived.

One paragraph: Affect is the directional rise or fall in the grading of an idea as a horizon is refined; conatus is the organized tendency to pursue such increases.

## Appendix summary

The eleven derived primitives show the main methodological promise of the book. Time, aboutness, viewpoint, feeling, contrast, reference, possibility, paradigm, belief, attraction, and self-reference do not need to be pasted onto the theory from outside. They can be reconstructed from the structure already doing the work.
