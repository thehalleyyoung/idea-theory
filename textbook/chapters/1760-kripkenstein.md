# Chapter 139 — Kripkenstein on Rule-Following

The rule following paradox begins with a disturbing thought. No finite history of using a word seems to determine exactly what rule you meant. If that is right, meaning cannot rest securely inside a private mind. It must be stabilized by practice, correction, and communal uptake.

## Biographical preamble

Kripkenstein names Saul Kripke's reading of Ludwig Wittgenstein, pronounced *VIT gen shtine*, especially in Wittgenstein on Rules and Private Language. Kripke lived from 1940 to 2022 and worked in analytic philosophy, logic, and language. Wittgenstein lived from 1889 to 1951 and wrote in the Austrian and British traditions. The thought, in Kripke's voice, is that no fact about an individual fixes which rule was meant.

## Position

The famous example contrasts plus with a deviant rule sometimes called quus. Your past uses of addition are finite. A skeptic can invent a different rule that agrees with all past answers but diverges on a new case. What fact proves that you meant ordinary addition rather than the deviant rule. Not an image in the mind, because any image needs interpretation. Not a formula, because the formula also needs a rule for its use. Not past behavior, because past behavior is finite.

Kripke calls this a skeptical paradox. His skeptical solution is not that meaning vanishes, but that meaning is not grounded in private facts. It is grounded in communal criteria for correct and incorrect use. We say someone follows a rule because their responses can be assessed within a shared practice.

## Algebraic re-statement

In Idea Theory, a rule is a pattern of composition across cases. Identity is the unchanged rule expectation before a new case is presented. The resonance pairing measures how strongly a proposed response fits the practice. A finite record preserves some applications but does not uniquely determine all future extensions.

The preservation part contains the past agreement. The suppression part contains rejected deviant extensions. The emergence part is the community stabilized rule, which is not reducible to any one person's inner episode. Degree or grading matters because a rule with greater complexity can be made to fit any finite sample, while a community usually favors lower degree patterns that remain teachable and correctable.

## Central claim as theorem

*Finite private use does not determine meaning unless communal horizons stabilize the rule.*

This is T13 read through Kripkenstein. The theorem rules out a purely private global meaning extracted from finite local data. It predicts that meaning becomes determinate only when local performances are embedded in a horizon where correction, training, and expectation glue them into a shared practice. Without that horizon, rival continuations remain possible.

Carefully restated, the horizon category contains learners, teachers, examples, corrections, future applications, and communal standards. The horizon functor assigns possible continuations to each local record of use. Composition extends a rule from case to case. Identity is no change in the accepted practice. The resonance pairing scores the fit between a response and the community standard. The preservation part keeps past applications, the suppression part rejects deviant continuations, and the emergence part is the socially stabilized rule. The diagnostic derived primitive is differentiation, because the rule separates correct from incorrect continuations.

## What formalism adds

The formalism separates underdetermination from collapse. The skeptic is right that finite local data do not force one unique global rule. But it does not follow that language cannot function. The horizon category supplies the missing structure. Public training, shared correction, and repeated uptake narrow possible continuations until one practice has practical authority.

This also explains why examples matter. A teacher does not transmit an infinite list. The teacher shapes resonance. The learner's next move is corrected, praised, repeated, or redirected. Over time, the preservation part expands, the suppression part grows sharper, and the emergence part becomes a reliable ability to go on in the same way.

A classroom makes the point gently. A child writes a series of numbers and seems to have learned counting by twos. Then the child continues in an unexpected way. The teacher does not inspect a hidden mental object called the rule. The teacher corrects, demonstrates, asks for repetition, and places the response among many other examples. The rule becomes determinate through this field of correction. The child's private confidence is not enough, and the teacher's authority alone is not magic. What matters is the developing resonance between examples, responses, and communal standards. That is why rule following is neither inner glow nor mere social bullying, but trained participation in a practice.

## Where it diverges

Kripke presents the paradox with radical skeptical force. Idea Theory softens that force by supplying a constructive social mechanism. It may therefore sound less skeptical than Kripke's Wittgenstein. The danger is to treat the community as a magical answer. Communities can be confused, divided, oppressive, or changing. The formalism must therefore treat communal stabilization as fallible horizon structure, not as guaranteed truth.

## Horizon structure

Kripkenstein's horizon is pedagogical and communal. It includes past examples, future cases, teachers, learners, sanctions, and shared forms of life. It most centrally instantiates T13, because local uses need a sheaf like gluing condition to become one rule. When gluing fails, we get indeterminacy, disagreement, or a split practice.

## Diagnostic derived primitives

The diagnostic derived primitives are differentiation, schema, and perspective. Differentiation marks correct from incorrect use. Schema names the pattern by which one goes on. Perspective names the difference between the private learner's seeming and the public practice's standard.

One more point follows. The community does not determine meaning by vote at each moment. It provides a durable horizon in which correction has authority and innovation can still be recognized as innovation. A joke, a metaphor, or a new technical use can bend the rule because there is already a practice for it to bend.

## Chapter summary

Kripkenstein teaches that meaning cannot be secured by a finite private history. Idea Theory restates the paradox as a failure of local data to determine a unique global rule. Communal practice supplies a horizon in which preservation, suppression, and emergence stabilize rule following. T13 is central because meaning arises when local uses glue into a public practice.
