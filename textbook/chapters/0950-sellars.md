# Chapter 75 — Wilfrid Sellars

This chapter treats Wilfrid Sellars as a test case for idea theory. We keep the operating
definitions audible: composition puts ideas together, the identity idea leaves a composition
unchanged, the resonance pairing measures directed evocation, the preservation part,
suppression part, and emergence part describe what is kept, cancelled, and newly produced,
and the horizon functor — the rule mathematicians label with the Greek letter xi — lets the
whole algebra vary by context.

## Biographical preamble

Wilfrid Sellars, pronounced approximately *SELL-erz* lived from 1912 to 1989, worked in the
United States, and belongs here to analytic philosophy and pragmatism. In one compressed
sentence, he said philosophy aims to understand how things hang together. That sentence
gives the chapter its entry point: meaning is not a bare object sitting before a spectator,
but a structured achievement within a field of acts, signs, practices, or horizons.

## Position

The position can be put simply. Sellars' Empiricism and the Philosophy of Mind attacks the
Myth of the Given: the doctrine that there is a class of cognitive episodes (sensory
experiences, sense-data) whose content is non-inferentially and indubitably available and
which can serve as the foundation of empirical knowledge. To know is to be able to justify,
and justification belongs to "the logical space of reasons", not to natural causation. The
manifest image (the world of persons) and the scientific image (the world of micro-
particulars) coexist and must be reconciled by a synoptic philosophy. The point is not
merely historical. It tells us what kind of item is being counted as an idea: not a loose
mental image, but a content whose sense depends on the way it is given, used, interpreted,
or carried forward.

## Algebraic re-statement

In the algebraic re-statement, the basic domain is the field of contents named by the zoo
entry. The space of reasons is an inferential graph: an item counts as known when it is
located among appropriate justifying neighbours. The myth of the given would posit items
that justify themselves without inferential connections; Sellars denies that such items can
play the role of knowledge. The reliability theory of perception becomes an additional
layer: a perceptual report is well grounded when a reliable causal chain runs from
environment to utterance, but the report enters the space of reasons only when the speaker
can in principle articulate that reliability. Concept possession is participation in
inferential practice, because a preservation relation is available only when a speaker
recognises the move from one idea to another as licit. The manifest and scientific images
are two partly overlapping sub-algebras, and the synoptic project constructs a translation
between them while preserving the irreducible features of personhood. Psychological
discourse about inner episodes is a theoretical posit modelled on overt linguistic
behaviour. Read aloud, this says that the thinker supplies a local model of the ten-tuple: a
domain of contents, a composition rule, an identity idea, directed resonance, the threefold
division into preservation, suppression, and emergence, an emergence cocycle, a degree of
articulation, and a context-sensitive horizon structure.

## Theorem re-statement

*The theorem-style claim is this: There is no non-empty subclass G of the idea domain whose members are justified independently of the emergence part; every justification is inferential.*

In paragraph form, the claim rules out the fantasy that meaning is already complete before
context enters. There is no non-empty subclass G of the idea domain whose members are
justified independently of the emergence part; every justification is inferential.
T-Sellars-2 (logical space of reasons). Knowledge is a status in the emergence part, not in
causation; the space of reasons is normative, not natural. T-Sellars-3 (concept possession
via inference). The prediction is that failures of understanding will not be accidental
noise. They will cluster where a local content cannot be carried unchanged across a change
of horizon, or where an apparent identity hides suppressed difference.

Carefully restated, the relevant idea algebra has a domain of contents, an identity idea,
associative composition, the resonance pairing, the preservation part, the suppression part,
the emergence part, the emergence cocycle, degree, the horizon category, and the horizon
functor. The central claim says that the philosopher's privileged object is stable only when
these roles cooperate. Where local data can be glued, the chapter instantiates the indexical pullback theorem; where
translation fails, the obstruction is also audible as a horizon-functor effect.

## What the formalism adds

What the formalism adds is clarification without replacement. The Myth of the Given is
identified as the postulation of a specific (and forbidden) sub-algebra, making the
polemical thesis precise. Justification and causation are explicitly separated as different
operators on the algebra, removing the slide between them that Sellars diagnoses.
Inferentialism about concepts is rendered as participation in the emergence part, connecting
Sellars to Brandom and to the wider algebraic picture. The two-images problem becomes a sub-
algebra-mapping problem, exposing what a successful synthesis would have to preserve. The
myth of Jones is captured as a theoretical posit with a determinate origin in linguistic
behaviour, clarifying its semi-fictional status. In ordinary language, the formalism lets us
ask which operation does the philosophical work. Is the key relation resonance, because one
content fulfills or evokes another? Is it suppression, because meaning appears through
contrast? Is it the horizon functor, because every local act changes when the context
changes? The answer differs from thinker to thinker, but the vocabulary stays stable.

## Where it diverges

The divergence is equally important. Sellars' Kantian background — the categories as
inferential roles inherited from a transcendental analytic — is gestured at by the emergence
part but not faithfully reconstructed. The synoptic vision is supposed to be philosophically
achieved, not merely posited; the algebra describes the goal but cannot perform it. The
treatment of sensory consciousness as ultimately requiring a "sensum" theory in late Sellars
(the "grain argument") cuts against a purely inferentialist reading the algebra encodes. The
dense, allusive style of Sellars — the appeal to "stereoscopic vision", the mythic
narratives — is part of his philosophical method and the algebra renders only the doctrine.
The historical embedding in Wittgensteinian, Carnapian, and pragmatist debates of mid-
century America gives Sellars' positions their force; isolated as theorems they look
thinner. This is not a defect in the reading. It marks the price of any formal
reconstruction: the algebra shows the load-bearing structure, while the original philosophy
often also includes tone, method, ethical urgency, religious practice, literary performance,
or first-person discipline.

## Horizon structure and the horizon-functor theorem

The horizon structure names the context in which the local algebra lives. Sellars' the
horizon category is the layered space of the manifest image, scientific image, linguistic
training, and normative inferential roles. Morphisms translate reports, observations, and
theoretical posits into the space of reasons, rejecting any given item that bypasses
conceptual uptake. schema or paradigm distinguishes manifest and scientific images,
perspective is the language-entry participant, and intentionality is norm-governed
classification rather than bare sensory givenness. This is the indexical pullback theorem: the Myth of the Given is the
false claim that sensory content pulls back into knowledge without inferential horizon-
translation. For this reason the chapter should be heard through the indexical pullback theorem. The hermeneutic sheaf theorem is the hermeneutic
sheaf theorem, where local meanings glue into a global one when they agree on overlaps. The indexical pullback theorem
is the indexical pullback theorem, where preservation and emergence fail to translate
cleanly. The profundity theorem is the profundity theorem, where depth comes from breadth across horizons and
closure under emergence.

## Derived primitives

The derived primitives make the diagnosis compact. schema or paradigm: The manifest and
scientific images are competing schema-components requiring stereoscopic integration. R. The
myth of the given is a reflexive critique of epistemology's own foundations. intentionality:
Meaning as functional role directs concepts through inferential placement in the space of
reasons. differentiation: Observational reports gain content by differential inferential
roles, not by bare givenness. None of these is introduced as an extra metaphysical
ingredient. Each is derived from the ten-tuple: intentionality from resonance across
horizons, temporality from ordered horizon-translation, perspective from a home region in
the horizon category, differentiation from suppression, and reflexion from self-application
of the horizon functor.

## Chapter summary

Wilfrid Sellars therefore enters idea theory as a disciplined local algebra of meaning. The
chapter has followed the promised path: biography, position, algebraic re-statement, theorem
re-statement, addition, divergence, horizon structure, and derived primitives. The result is
not a replacement for the thinker, but a listening guide to the formal pressure already
present in the work.
