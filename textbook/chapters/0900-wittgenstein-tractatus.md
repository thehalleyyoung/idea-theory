# Chapter 70 — Early Wittgenstein

This chapter treats Early Wittgenstein as a test case for idea theory. We keep the operating
definitions audible: composition puts ideas together, the identity idea leaves a composition
unchanged, the resonance pairing measures directed evocation, the preservation part,
suppression part, and emergence part describe what is kept, cancelled, and newly produced,
and the horizon functor — the rule mathematicians label with the Greek letter xi — lets the
whole algebra vary by context.

## Biographical preamble

Ludwig Wittgenstein, pronounced approximately *LOOD-vig VIT-gen-shtine* lived from 1889 to
1951, worked in Austria and Britain, and belongs here to early analytic philosophy. In one
compressed sentence, he wrote that the limits of language mean the limits of the world. That
sentence gives the chapter its entry point: meaning is not a bare object sitting before a
spectator, but a structured achievement within a field of acts, signs, practices, or
horizons.

## Position

The position can be put simply. The Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus (1921) presents an
austere picture: the world is the totality of facts, not of things; facts are configurations
of objects; elementary propositions are pictures of atomic facts; molecular propositions are
truth-functional combinations of elementary ones. Logic takes care of itself: the rules of
inference are tautologies that say nothing about the world. The picture theory of meaning
grounds the proposition's representational power: a proposition shares with the fact it
pictures a logical form, and depicting consists in representing one structure by another
with the same logical form. To understand a proposition is to grasp this isomorphism.
Logical analysis decomposes complex propositions into their elementary constituents. The
point is not merely historical. It tells us what kind of item is being counted as an idea:
not a loose mental image, but a content whose sense depends on the way it is given, used,
interpreted, or carried forward.

## Algebraic re-statement

In the algebraic re-statement, the basic domain is the field of contents named by the zoo
entry. Take the idea domain to be the set of elementary propositions (and their truth-
functional combinations): items insofar as they picture atomic facts. Each element is tagged
by its truth-functional decomposition and by the elementary constituents from which it is
built. The identity idea is the tautology: the proposition true under every truth-
assignment, contributing no information. Composition is truth-functional composition:
conjunction, disjunction, negation, conditional. composition is governed entirely by the
propositional calculus; no extra-logical principles enter. The algebra is in this sense
maximally clean. Read aloud, this says that the thinker supplies a local model of the ten-
tuple: a domain of contents, a composition rule, an identity idea, directed resonance, the
threefold division into preservation, suppression, and emergence, an emergence cocycle, a
degree of articulation, and a context-sensitive horizon structure.

## Theorem re-statement

*The theorem-style claim is this: The first theorem-shaped claim is that the world is flat: the preservation part is 0 everywhere.*

In paragraph form, the claim rules out the fantasy that meaning is already complete before
context enters. The first theorem-shaped claim is that the world is flat: the preservation
part is 0 everywhere. All logical structure is tautological; logic adds nothing to the world
but exhibits the form already there. This is a the meaning curvature theorem claim with negative valence: the algebra
has no irreducible curvature. The second claim is that truth-functions are composition: the
only legitimate composition is truth-functional, and any apparent non-truth-functional
combination is either further analyzable into truth-functional form or is nonsensical. The
prediction is that failures of understanding will not be accidental noise. They will cluster
where a local content cannot be carried unchanged across a change of horizon, or where an
apparent identity hides suppressed difference.

Carefully restated, the relevant idea algebra has a domain of contents, an identity idea,
associative composition, the resonance pairing, the preservation part, the suppression part,
the emergence part, the emergence cocycle, degree, the horizon category, and the horizon
functor. The central claim says that the philosopher's privileged object is stable only when
these roles cooperate. Where local data can be glued, the chapter instantiates the indexical pullback theorem; where
translation fails, the obstruction is also audible as a horizon-functor effect.

## What the formalism adds

What the formalism adds is clarification without replacement. First, the picture theory
becomes the claim that the resonance pairing is maximal when the proposition is true — a
precise correspondence theory in which truth is structural isomorphism between proposition
and fact. Second, the preservation part is 0 makes explicit the claim that logic adds
nothing to the world. The algebra of the Tractatus is structurally unique among the
philosopher zoo: it is the only one that requires zero curvature throughout, and the
requirement has dramatic consequences. Third, the showing/saying distinction becomes the
structural distinction between what is internal to the algebra (the elements and their
compositions, which can be said) and what is internal to the algebra's form (the categorial
constraints on composition and the type structure, which cannot be said but show themselves
in the algebra's operation). Fourth, the limits of language correspond to the boundary of
the idea domain. In ordinary language, the formalism lets us ask which operation does the
philosophical work. Is the key relation resonance, because one content fulfills or evokes
another? Is it suppression, because meaning appears through contrast? Is it the horizon
functor, because every local act changes when the context changes? The answer differs from
thinker to thinker, but the vocabulary stays stable.

## Where it diverges

The divergence is equally important. First, the showing/saying distinction — what shows
itself but cannot be said — corresponds to what is outside the idea domain but constitutive
of it; the formalism has no native way to represent this. The structural form of the algebra
shows in its operation, but the algebra cannot describe its own form from within. Second,
the picture theory presupposes a notion of logical form shared between proposition and fact
that the algebra does not internally contain. The algebra's elements are propositional;
facts are extra-algebraic, and the isomorphism that gives meaning lives at the interface.
Third, the elementary propositions on which the entire structure rests were never actually
exhibited by Wittgenstein; their existence is a postulate. This is not a defect in the
reading. It marks the price of any formal reconstruction: the algebra shows the load-bearing
structure, while the original philosophy often also includes tone, method, ethical urgency,
religious practice, literary performance, or first-person discipline.

## Horizon structure and the horizon-functor theorem

The horizon structure names the context in which the local algebra lives. The early
Wittgenstein's the horizon category is the austere picture-theoretic space of facts,
elementary propositions, logical form, and the limit of language. Morphisms project
propositions onto possible states of affairs when they share pictorial form; ethical,
aesthetic, and metaphysical utterances lack such admissible projection. rigid reference
binds names to objects, schema or paradigm is logical space, and R appears when the book's
own propositions must be thrown away. This is the indexical pullback theorem: what can be shown does not pull back into
sayable propositions, so metaphysical sense fails at the boundary of the horizon category.
For this reason the chapter should be heard through the indexical pullback theorem. The hermeneutic sheaf theorem is the hermeneutic sheaf
theorem, where local meanings glue into a global one when they agree on overlaps. The indexical pullback theorem is the
indexical pullback theorem, where preservation and emergence fail to translate cleanly. The profundity theorem
is the profundity theorem, where depth comes from breadth across horizons and closure under
emergence.

## Derived primitives

The derived primitives make the diagnosis compact. rigid reference: Names stand for simple
objects, providing rigid anchors within logical space. schema or paradigm: Logical space is
the schema of possible states of affairs that propositions partition. intentionality: The
picture relation makes propositions intentionally about facts by sharing logical form. R.
The ladder is reflexive: the book shows its own propositions must finally be thrown away.
None of these is introduced as an extra metaphysical ingredient. Each is derived from the
ten-tuple: intentionality from resonance across horizons, temporality from ordered horizon-
translation, perspective from a home region in the horizon category, differentiation from
suppression, and reflexion from self-application of the horizon functor.

## Chapter summary

Early Wittgenstein therefore enters idea theory as a disciplined local algebra of meaning.
The chapter has followed the promised path: biography, position, algebraic re-statement,
theorem re-statement, addition, divergence, horizon structure, and derived primitives. The
result is not a replacement for the thinker, but a listening guide to the formal pressure
already present in the work.
