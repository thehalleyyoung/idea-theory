# Chapter 68 — Gottlob Frege

This chapter treats Gottlob Frege as a test case for idea theory. We keep the operating
definitions audible: composition puts ideas together, the identity idea leaves a composition
unchanged, the resonance pairing measures directed evocation, the preservation part,
suppression part, and emergence part describe what is kept, cancelled, and newly produced,
and the horizon functor — the rule mathematicians label with the Greek letter xi — lets the
whole algebra vary by context.

## Biographical preamble

Gottlob Frege, pronounced approximately *GOT-lohb FRAY-guh* lived from 1848 to 1925, worked
in Germany, and belongs here to logic and early analytic philosophy. In one compressed
sentence, he taught that words have meaning only in the context of a proposition. That
sentence gives the chapter its entry point: meaning is not a bare object sitting before a
spectator, but a structured achievement within a field of acts, signs, practices, or
horizons.

## Position

The position can be put simply. Frege founds modern logic with the Begriffsschrift (1879), a
concept-script that supersedes Aristotelian syllogistic with quantification, function-
argument structure, and a rigorous notation for proof. The aim is not new in spirit but
unprecedented in execution: a logic adequate to the inferential structure of mathematics.
The Grundlagen der Arithmetik (1884) and Grundgesetze (1893/1903) pursue the logicist
thesis: arithmetic is reducible to pure logic. Numbers are not abstractions from collections
of physical objects but logical objects, defined as extensions of concepts. The natural
numbers are constructed in the logic itself, with no appeal to intuition. The point is not
merely historical. It tells us what kind of item is being counted as an idea: not a loose
mental image, but a content whose sense depends on the way it is given, used, interpreted,
or carried forward.

## Algebraic re-statement

In the algebraic re-statement, the basic domain is the field of contents named by the zoo
entry. Take the idea domain to be the set of senses (Sinne): items as modes of presentation
of references, finer-grained than references themselves and capable of being grasped by
thought. Each element is tagged by its category (object-sense, concept-sense, relation-
sense) and by its logical complexity. The identity idea is a designator-position that admits
any sense of the appropriate type — a logical placeholder for substitution. Composition is
functional application: a concept-sense composed with an object-sense yields a thought-sense
(the sense of the resulting sentence). composition is type-respecting: only senses of
compatible categories can be composed, and the result inherits a determinate type from the
constituents. The resonance pairing is co-reference: the resonance pairing when the content
under discussion and the relevant content have the same Bedeutung. Read aloud, this
says that the thinker supplies a local model of the ten-tuple: a domain of contents, a
composition rule, an identity idea, directed resonance, the threefold division into
preservation, suppression, and emergence, an emergence cocycle, a degree of articulation,
and a context-sensitive horizon structure.

## Theorem re-statement

*The theorem-style claim is this: The first theorem-shaped claim is that sense is the primary element of the idea domain; reference is the value of the emergence part.*

In paragraph form, the claim rules out the fantasy that meaning is already complete before
context enters. The first theorem-shaped claim is that sense is the primary element of the
idea domain; reference is the value of the emergence part. The algebra operates on senses;
references are derived. This is a T6 claim with the further content that the same reference
can be reached by many distinct senses. The second claim is that co-reference is the
equivalence relation induced by the resonance pairing. The prediction is that failures of
understanding will not be accidental noise. They will cluster where a local content cannot
be carried unchanged across a change of horizon, or where an apparent identity hides
suppressed difference.

Carefully restated, the relevant idea algebra has a domain of contents, an identity idea,
associative composition, the resonance pairing, the preservation part, the suppression part,
the emergence part, the emergence cocycle, degree, the horizon category, and the horizon
functor. The central claim says that the philosopher's privileged object is stable only when
these roles cooperate. Where local data can be glued, the chapter instantiates T15; where
translation fails, the obstruction is also audible as a horizon-functor effect.

## What the formalism adds

What the formalism adds is clarification without replacement. First, the sense-reference
distinction becomes a fibre-bundle structure: the projection is the relevant formal role, and
senses are fibres over references. Different senses over the same reference are elements
with the resonance pairing but with its location in the idea domain. The pictorial
geometry of fibres makes Frege's distinction structurally vivid. Second, the
compositionality of sense becomes the algebraic claim that composition preserves the
relevant structure: the sense of a complex expression is determined by the senses of its
parts and the way they are combined. The Fregean slogan gains an algebraic correlate. In
ordinary language, the formalism lets us ask which operation does the philosophical work. Is
the key relation resonance, because one content fulfills or evokes another? Is it
suppression, because meaning appears through contrast? Is it the horizon functor, because
every local act changes when the context changes? The answer differs from thinker to
thinker, but the vocabulary stays stable.

## Where it diverges

The divergence is equally important. First, Frege's Context Principle (only in sentence
context does a word have reference) means composition must be holistic; the formalism allows
any composition, which is too liberal. The Fregean discipline of analyzing top-down rather
than bottom-up is hard to enforce structurally. Second, the third realm of senses —
abstract, mind-independent, eternal — has a Platonist ontology the algebra is silent about.
the idea domain is a structural object; whether senses inhabit a third realm is a
metaphysical question outside the structural facts. Third, the strict distinction between
concept (unsaturated, function-like) and object (saturated, argument-like) is more delicate
than the algebra's category-tagging captures. This is not a defect in the reading. It marks
the price of any formal reconstruction: the algebra shows the load-bearing structure, while
the original philosophy often also includes tone, method, ethical urgency, religious
practice, literary performance, or first-person discipline.

## Horizon structure and the horizon-functor theorem

The horizon structure names the context in which the local algebra lives. Frege's the
horizon category is the logical space of senses, references, functions, arguments, and
judgment-strokes, sharply separated from psychology. Morphisms translate ordinary
propositions into Begriffsschrift, preserving inferential articulation while stripping away
subjective association. rigid reference is reference, intentionality is sense as mode of
presentation, and differentiation separates concept and object by type-theoretic role rather
than empirical contrast. This is T15: a thought's objectivity is its trans-horizonal
stability across thinkers, languages, and presentations once expressed in logical form. For
this reason the chapter should be heard through T15. T13 is the hermeneutic sheaf theorem,
where local meanings glue into a global one when they agree on overlaps. T14 is the
indexical pullback theorem, where preservation and emergence fail to translate cleanly. T15
is the profundity theorem, where depth comes from breadth across horizons and closure under
emergence.

## Derived primitives

The derived primitives make the diagnosis compact. rigid reference: Bedeutung supplies
reference, with proper names rigidly aiming at objects rather than senses. intentionality:
Sinn is the mode of presentation through which a reference is intentionally given.
differentiation: The concept/object distinction differentiates predicative functions from
saturated objects. credence: Truth-values are objects that stabilize judgment's credence
target as the True or the False. None of these is introduced as an extra metaphysical
ingredient. Each is derived from the ten-tuple: intentionality from resonance across
horizons, temporality from ordered horizon-translation, perspective from a home region in
the horizon category, differentiation from suppression, and reflexion from self-application
of the horizon functor.

## Chapter summary

Gottlob Frege therefore enters idea theory as a disciplined local algebra of meaning. The
chapter has followed the promised path: biography, position, algebraic re-statement, theorem
re-statement, addition, divergence, horizon structure, and derived primitives. The result is
not a replacement for the thinker, but a listening guide to the formal pressure already
present in the work.
