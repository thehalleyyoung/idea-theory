# Chapter 36 — Avicenna

Ibn Sina, pronounced approximately *IBN see-NAH*, known in Latin as Avicenna, lived from 980 to 1037 in the Persian and Central Asian Islamic philosophical world. In his own translated words: “The Necessary Existent has no quiddity other than that he is necessary existent.”

## The position in its own voice

Hear the doctrine in its own intellectual voice. Avicenna distinguishes essence from existence: an essence considered in itself is neutral with respect to its instantiation, and existence is something added to essence by the act of a cause, ultimately the Necessary Existent whose essence is identical with its existence. Universals are common natures considered in the mind, which exist in things only as individuated by matter and accidents. Knowledge proceeds by the agent intellect's illumination, which actualises intelligibles in the receptive human intellect. The voice trains attention by asking what is stable, what must be discarded, and what kind of insight appears only after that discipline has been accepted.

## The idea algebra sub-instance

Before translating, recall the operating definitions. Ideas compose with other ideas. Composition has a preservation part, a suppression part, and an emergence part. Ideas resonate with one another, and their non-reciprocal pull is meaning curvature. Ideas also live over horizons: contexts, stages, perspectives, times, practices, or worlds. Avicenna treats essences as intelligible contents that can be considered before actuality. Composition with existence makes a contingent essence actual through cause. The preservation part is the essence that remains across mental and external consideration. The suppression part removes accidents when intellect abstracts, and the emergence part is actual being in the caused order. Resonance is causal and intelligible dependence on the Necessary Existent. Degree marks ascent from image to universal intelligible. In plain English, the sub-instance asks what counts as an idea, how ideas combine, what survives, what is cancelled, and what new content appears.

## Central claim as theorem

*If contingent essences do not contain their own existence, then actuality requires a necessary ground whose essence and existence are inseparable.*

The theorem rules out the easy opposite of the view. It predicts that across the proper horizons some content will travel, some will fail, and some will arise only through that failure. The theorem identifies the middle structure: invariant, obstruction, or gluing.

Carefully restated, choose the relevant horizon category and let the horizon functor - the rule mathematicians label with the Greek letter xi - assign a complete idea algebra to each horizon. Name composition, identity, resonance, the preservation part, the suppression part, the emergence part, the emergence cocycle, degree, and meaning curvature. Then the theorem says that Avicenna's central claim is exactly the stable support, failed pullback, or glued agreement described in the preceding paragraph, stated without symbols.

## What the formalism adds

It separates "what is" from "whether is" rigorously, replacing potentially confused medieval debates with two distinct objects: the essence and the existence predicate . The agent intellect becomes a function with a domain and a range, allowing one to ask precisely which sensibles fall under it and what fails when the receptive intellect is malformed. The threefold consideration of universals becomes three rigorously defined projections rather than a metaphysical mystery, clarifying what is shared across the modes.

A second gain is the treatment of self-awareness. The floating person is not introduced as a curiosity but as a test case for reflexion: strip away bodily and sensory horizons, and self-presence still remains. A third gain is the handling of universals. The same nature can be considered in itself, in things, and in the intellect without becoming three unrelated items. The horizon structure lets those considerations be distinguished while preserving the intelligible content that travels among them. More generally, the formalism adds comparison. It tells the listener whether preservation, suppression, or emergence is carrying the doctrine.

This also keeps the metaphysical drama intact for the listener. Avicenna is not asking whether a word has a definition only; he is asking why any definable thing is actual at all. The algebraic restatement lets essence remain intelligible while making existence a received act, dependent on a horizon of causation that culminates in necessity.

## Where the formalism diverges

Avicenna's existence is not a predicate added to essence in the Fregean sense; the algebra's risks treating it as such, flattening the act-of-being into a Boolean. The doctrine of essences as natures considered in themselves , neutral to universality and particularity, resists capture: equivalence classes are still in some context, while Avicennian essence-in-itself is strictly contextless. Emanation is for Avicenna a metaphysical procession with theological weight; The algebra is therefore an aid to hearing, not a substitute for reading.

## Horizon category and horizon-functor theorem

Avicenna's the horizon category orders essence, existence, intellect, imagination, and emanative cosmology. Morphisms translate an essence considered in itself into its instantiation under existence, or from sensible images toward intelligible forms received from the Active Intellect. potentiality captures possible essence awaiting existential realisation, intentionality structures intellection, and schema distinguishes the necessary horizon of God from the contingent horizons of creatures. In this chapter the most central horizon-functor theorem is the Profundity theorem. It names gluing, obstruction, or depth as the central horizon behavior.

## Diagnostic derived primitives

potentiality. The possible in itself, mumkin al-wujūd, is potential existence requiring a cause for actuality. rigid reference. The Necessary Existent is a rigid section whose essence and existence are identical in every horizon. reflexion. The floating man reveals reflexive self-awareness prior to bodily or sensory mediation. schema. The emanated intelligences form ordered cosmological schema-components between the Necessary Existent and sublunary souls. These primitives are consequences of the way composition, resonance, degree, and horizon translation behave here.

One more listening cue helps. Ask, at every turn, whether the thinker is protecting a stable reference, exposing a failed translation, or gathering scattered meanings into a whole. That question keeps the formal reconstruction close to the philosophical voice rather than letting it become a merely external classification.

## Chapter summary

Avicenna gives one answer to what an idea is, how it combines, and how it remains or fails to remain meaningful across horizons. The idea algebra restates that answer by naming preserved content, suppressed illusion, emergent insight, horizon behavior, and diagnostic derived primitives. The gain is clarity; the cost is abstraction. A good listener keeps both in view.
